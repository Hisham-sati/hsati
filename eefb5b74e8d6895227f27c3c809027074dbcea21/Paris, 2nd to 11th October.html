  • GLr/GLr−s does not give us s-frames. Woops. The point is that U(n)/U(r−s) gives us partial orthonormal frames, but this is because there is sort of a lemma that a partial orthonormal frame 'already contains the information of a complement'. Also note that the dimensions aren't equal, e.g. dim(GLr/GL1)=r2−1, but the space of (r−1)-frames (without a choice of complement) has dimension r(r−1)=r2−r

Back to principal bundles

  • Linear connections (also called Koszul connections) on a vector bundle E are equivalent to principal GLr-connections on the corresponding principal GLr-bundle P of frames, which are defined to be (for a principal GLr-bundle P) 𝔤𝔩r-valued 1-forms on P that are GLr-equivariant and reproduce the 𝔤𝔩r-generators of the fundamental vector fields on P, i.e. a connection is given by some connection form
    ω∈Ω1P⊗𝔤𝔩r
    such that
    • Adg(R∗gω)=ω for all g∈GLr
    • ω(ξ#)=ξ for all ξ∈𝔤𝔩r.
  • The first condition above is the commutativity of the diagram
    TpPRg↓Tp⋅gP−→ω−→ω𝔤𝔩r↓Adg−1𝔤𝔩r
  • (There is a more general equivalence between principal G-connections and G-equivariant Ehresmann connections.)
  • The curvature form is given by
    Ω=dω+12[ω,ω]∈Ω2P⊗𝔤𝔩r
    where the Lie bracket is that of 𝔤𝔩r (recall that we always take the wedge product on the form part) (N.B. since we have a matrix Lie group, we can really think of [−,−] as the Lie bracket, and we have, for forms α,β of degree p,q resp., [α,β]=αβ−(−1)pqβα).
  • Lemma. Both ω and Ω are 𝔤𝔩r-equivariant; ω is vertical and Ω is horizontal.
  • (Second) structure equation. Dω=Ω.
  • (Second) Bianchi identity. DΩ=0.

Day 1

Leibnitz rule

  • Justifying the Leibnitz rule on si=M⋅(δ0iδ1i) is done element-wise: note two important things:
    • the order is ∇(fs)=f∇(s)+df⊗s, i.e. f is always on the left;
    • we shouldn't really write dM⊗(δ0iδ1i) because the tensor product should be between forms and endomorphisms, but dM is both... so for the moment I'll write ⊗˜ to mean 'it's the tensor product but the terms are kind of mixed up'
  • When we do Leibnitz properly, we get dM⊗˜M−1si, which gives us (dM)M−1, which, since we take the trace (and so have cyclic commutativity), gives us the M−1dM that we know and love

Local sections and intrinsic-ness

  • For the moment, we do take local sections of our vector bundle, because otherwise we can't really do any calculations! Even when you just write that the intrinsic sections are given by 'the i-th column of the representative matrix' you are implicitly (and sneakily) using local sections.
  • Eventually we hope to find something that doesn't need local sections, but let's take this one step at a time...
  • We should formalise this as follows: the local sections siα for E→X give us local sections of ρ∗E→AEk, and then the canonical ones are defined as, over a point (x,[M]) (writing a point like this means that we have trivialised E — this is the sneaky use of local sections) as the image of the siα under M, i.e. the i-th column of M, since, again, just to be clear, we are using the siα to trivialise our bundle.
  • Remember that, when we say a G-torsor is isomorphic to G, we are picking a trivialisation.

What AEk really is

  • AEk:=P×GLrWrn−k+1, i.e. the equivalence classes are given by the action of GLr, not SLn; we are using GLr instead of U(r) (i.e. 'not normalised') and so it sort of seems natural.
  • Much 'better' to think of AEk as P/GLk−1 (so, for example, points in A are not given by pairs (x,[M]) unless we choose a local trivialisation).
  • AEk is not a principal bundle over X, but just a fibre bundle; it is, however, a GL(k−1)?-principal bundle over the space of rank-(k−1)? quotients of E (or, at least, some statement like this is true, but we don't really care so much).
  • AEk is more than just the space of (r−k+1)-frames: it is the choice of such frames plus a complement. This means, in particular, that the bit where we say 'assume that there are some never-colinear sections of ρ∗E ' is unnecessary, and also that extending the trivial connection on the trivial sub-bundle to one on the whole of ρ∗E is conceptually much clearer: it is equivalent to picking a connection on the complement. Primarily, pulling back E over AEk is exactly the same as obtaining a (canonical) direct-sum decomposition ρ∗E≅Tr−k+1AEk⊕V — the trivial sub-bundle is exactly the span of the intrinsic si.
