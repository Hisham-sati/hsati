Setup

  • π:E→X a holomorphic rank-r bundle;
  • f:Fk→X the bundle of (r−k+1)-frames of E, with fibre Wk homotopic to the Stiefel manifold Wnk;
  • the Deligne complex
    ℤ(p)=(2πi)pℤ↪X−→dΩ1X−→d…−→dΩp−1X
    where (2πi)pℤ is in degree zero;
  • sheaves ℱp on X for p∈{0,…,2k} where
    ℱp(U)={σ:U×Δp→U×Wk≅f−1(U)∣fσ(x,t)=x} ;
  • homologically-graded complex
    𝒯∙=ℤX↪ℱ2k−1/ℱ2k−→∂ℱ2k−2−→∂…−→∂ℱ0
    where ℱ0 is in degree zero and ∂ is the usual boundary operator from the singular chain complex; the inclusion of ℤ into (ℱ2k−1/ℱ2k)(U) comes from the fact that kernel (under ∂) of the latter calculates the singular homology of Wk, which we know to be isomorphic to ℤ;
  • more generally, the local homology (i.e. the homology of a chain complex) is exactly the singular homology of the fibre, i.e.
    ℋi(𝒯∙)≅Hi(Wk;ℤ) for i∈{1,…,2k−1}
    and so 𝒯∙ is exact except at degree zero, giving us a quasi-isomorphism
    𝒯∙−→∼ℤX[0] ;
  • we have an element τ∈ℍ0(X;𝒯∙) corresponding to 1∈ℤ under the following chain of isomorphisms:
    ℤ≅H0(Wk;ℤ)≅ℍ0(Wk;ℤX[0])≅ℍ0(X;𝒯∙).

Transgression

  • We can transgress the kth Chern class of E to an element η∙∈Tot2k−1(𝒞ˇ∙(Ω∙f∗E)) which is concentrated in

    • 𝒞ˇ0(Ω2k−1f∗E) when E admits a global holomorphic connection;
    • 𝒞ˇ0(Ω2k−1f∗E)⊕𝒞ˇ1(Ω2k−2f∗E)⊕…⊕𝒞ˇk−1(Ωkf∗E) when E does not admit a global holomorphic connection.

    That is, Dη∙ is a lift of f∗ck to the Čech-de Rham complex, where D=d±δˇ.

  • η∙ is given by

    ∫Δ∙Pk(Θ∙f∗∇∙E,…,Θ∙f∗∇∙E,∙)
    where ∙=f∗∇∙E−∇∙triv, and ∇ptriv=∑pi=0ti∇triv,αi with ∇triv,αi trivial on the rank-(r−k+1) subbundle of f∗E.

  • Importantly, when restricted to the fibre Wk, the form η∙ is the canonical generator of H2k−1(Wk;ℤ). I'm still slightly unclear on how to prove this. (!) Also, Dη∙=f∗ck, and so any integral of Dη∙ over a cycle contained purely in the fibre must be zero, since Dη∙ is the pullback of a form on the base.

Fibre integration

  • Generally, given a form ω of degree d on some U×G, and a p-cycle σ:U×Δp→G, we define fibre integration of ω over σ by
    ∫σω=∫Δpσ̃ ∗ω
    where σ̃ :U×Δp→U×G is given by the projection to U in the first coordinate, and σ in the second. The resulting form is of degree (d−p) on U, taken to be zero in the case p>d.

Goals

  1. Construct a map (i.e. degree-wise maps, but not necessarily giving a morphism of complexes) 𝒯∙→Tot∙(𝒞ˇ∙(ℤ(2k)[2k])) given by integrating cycles over some transgression of the k-th Chern class of E.
  2. Show that, if E admits a global holomorphic connection, then this is indeed a morphism of complexes, and gives us a morphism 𝒯∙→ℍ2k(X;ℤ(2k)).
  3. Show that, if E does not admit a global holomorphic connection, then this fails to be a morphism of complexes (and that the obstruction is given exactly by the k-th Atiyah class), but does give us a morphism of complexes 𝒯∙→Tot∙(𝒞ˇ∙(ℤ(k)[2k])) and thus a morphism in T∙→ℍ2k(X;ℤ(k)).
  4. Explain why the image of τ in Deligne cohomology (in either case) under the respective morphism deserves to be called the kth Chern class.

The global case

  • Our transgression form η∙ is concentrated entirely in 𝒞ˇ0(Ω2k−1f∗E), and so we can think of it as a collection of forms ηα on f−1(Uα)≅Uα×Wk.
  • Fibre integration gives us a map
    ∫(−)(ηα)α∈I:ℱp→𝒞ˇ0(Ω2k−1−pX)
    for p∈{0,…,2k}, and we have the multiplication map ℤ→(2πi)pℤ, so to show that this gives a morphism of complexes we need to prove two things:
    1. fibre integration descends to a well-defined map on the quotient ℱ2k−1/ℱ2k;
    2. the squares all commute, i.e.
      D∘(∫(−)(ηα)α∈I)=(∫(−)(ηα)α∈I)∘d𝒯.
  • Lemma. Let Z∈ℱp for some p∈ℕ. Then
    D∫Z(ηα)α∈I=∫Z(Dηα)α∈I+∫∂Z(ηα)α∈I
    i.e. Stoke's theorem has a correction term. Note that this lemma is more general than stated here: it holds true for any form — not just η. In fact, for our specific η, using the fact that (Dηα)α∈I is a pullback of a form on the base, and that ∂Z is a cycle contained entirely in the fibre, we see that the correction term is actually zero, i.e.
    D∫Z(ηα)α∈I=∫∂Z(ηα)α∈I
  • Corollary 1. All the squares, except for the first, commute.
  • Corollary 2. Fibre integration descends to a well-defined map on the quotient ℱ2k−1/ℱ2k.
    Proof. Write [Z]=Z+∂Y∈ℱ2k−1/ℱ2k. Then
    ∫Z+∂Y(ηα)α∈I=∫Z(ηα)α∈I+D∫Y(ηα)α∈I−∫Y(Dηα)α∈I
    but the second term on the right-hand side is zero, because the integral of a (2k−1)-form over a (2k)-cycle is zero; and the third term on the right-hand side is zero, because (again) we are integrating the pullback of a form on the base over a cycle in the fibre.
  • For the first square, it suffices to show that
    ∫1(ηα)α∈I=(2πi)p.
    But since ηα is the fundamental generator of H2k−1(X;ℤ, and 1∈ℱ2k−1/ℱ2k corresponds to the fundamental generator of H2k−1(X;ℤ), Poincaré duality (compactness of the fibre? (!)) tells us that this integral is exactly 1. So do we need our morphism to really be fibre integration multiplied by some constant? (!).
  • Theorem.
    ∫τ(ηα)α∈I∈ℍ2k(X;ℤ(2k))
    is the kth Chern class.
