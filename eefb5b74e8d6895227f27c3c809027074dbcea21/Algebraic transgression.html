Algebraic transgression

SEE Paris, 2nd to 11th October

Basically, we can use Huybrecht's proof of 4.4.6 in Complex Geometry (see below) with compatible families of admissible simplicial connections (see Simplicial connections and other conventions), but the line bundle case works nicely by hand, as follows.

Motto. We transgress ck of a rank-r bundle to H2k−1(Wn,n−k+1;ℤ); see Stiefel manifolds.

Line bundles (a toy example)

Transition maps POV

Pull back our line bundle E to the space of 1-frames in ℂ, which is just GL1(ℂ). The local sections sα:x↦(x,σα(x)) of E pull back to

π∗sα:Uα×GL1(x,m)→(Uα×GL1)×ℂ×↦((x,m),σα(x))

(N.B. π here is the map from the associated bundle of 1-frames to X — should really change the notation to p or something less misleading...)
but we also have the global section
s:Uα×GL1(x,m)→(Uα×GL1)×ℂ×↦((x,m),m).

Since sβ=gαβsα we see that

π∗gαβ=π∗sβπ∗sα=(π∗sβ)/s(π∗sα)/s
where we note that π∗sα is just a section, but (π∗sα)/s is a true function.

Then atE=g−1αβdgαβ∈ˇ1(Ω1) is such that π∗atE=δˇdlog(π∗sα/s). This is our transgression.

Further, we can recover the generator of homology on the fibre as follows. The function π∗sα/s acts as (x,m)↦((x,m),1/m) (since we can assume that σα(x)=1 for some α) and so

dlog(π∗sα/s)=dlog(1/z)=d(1/z)(1/z)=−dzz=±d(reiθ)reiθ=1rdr+idθ.

Connections POV

Since π∗E is trivial we have a global connection ∇. Then

π∗(∇β−∇α)=(π∗∇β−∇)−(π∗∇α−∇)=:ϑβ−ϑα=δˇϑα
for some Čech-1-cochain ϑα of a 1-form, since the difference of two connections is a 1-form.

Now, the sα are taken to be ∇α-flat, and s is (trivially) ∇-flat, whence, assuming (wlog) that σα(x)=1,

sα:(x,m)↦((x,m),1)=((x,m),z⋅1/z)=1zs.

Then, by Leibnitz and flatness,
ϑαsα=−∇sα=−∇(1zs)=−1z∇(s)−s⊗d(1/z)=sd(1/z)=−zdzz2=−dzz.

Simplicial proof

We can adapt the proof in Huybrecht's (4.4.1 to 4.4.6) if we can show the following five statements are true.

  1. The difference of two simplicial connections is an admissible endomorphism-valued simplicial 1-form.
  2. K(∇∙+A∙)=K(∇∙)+A∙∧A∙+∇∙(A∙).
  3. P̃ (K(∇∙+A∙))=P̃ (K(∇∙))+ktP(K(∇∙),…,K(∇∙),∇∙(A∙))+(t2)
  4. ∇∙(K(∇∙)) (the Bianchi identity).
  5. dP(γ1∙,…,γk∙)=∑kj=1(−1)signP(γ1∙,…,∇∙(γj∙),…,γk∙).

Let's prove them.

  1. This is true if we have a compatible family of admissible simplicial connections.
  2. This works level-by-level, and that our simplicial connection is admissible ensures that all the forms are admissible endomorphism-valued simplicial forms.
  3. As above.
  4. As above.
  5. As above, as well as the fact that P̃  acts on the form parts by wedging, and the wedge of two simplicial forms is again simplicial.
