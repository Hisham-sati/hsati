Simplicial connections and other conventions

Simplices

  • Δ is the simplex category
    • the objects are the finite ordinals [n];
    • the morphisms the order-preserving maps;
    • we have the coface maps
      fin:[n−1]→[n]
      and codegeneracy maps
      sni:[n]→[n−1].
  • Δ[n]:=HomΔ(−,[n]) is the abstract n-simplex and is a simplicial set.
  • Δ∙:[n]↦ΔnTop is the collection of topological simplices and is a cosimplicial set, and we write Δn:=Δ∙([n])=ΔnTop, where ΔnTop⊂ℝn+1 has coordinates (t0,…,tn) such that all ti are non-negative and ∑iti=1.
  • The coface and codegeneracy maps give us maps on (co)simpicial spaces:
    • simplicial spaces Y∙ have face maps
      φni:=Y∙fin:Yn→Yn−1
      and degeneracy maps
      σin:=Y∙sni:Yn−1→Yn;
    • cosimplicial spaces Z∙ have coface maps
      fin:=Z∙fin:Zn−1→Zn
      and codegeneracy maps
      sni:=z∙sni:Zn→Zn−1.

Simplicial sheaves

  • A simplicial sheaf ∙ on a simplicial space Y∙ is the data of a collection of sheaves
    ∙={p on Yp}
    together with morphisms
    ∙α:(Y∙α)∗p→q
    for all α:[p]→[q] (n.b. these are covariant!) such that ∙(β∘α)=∙(β)∘∙(α).
  • A simplicial vector bundle is simply a simplicial sheaf that is locally free at each simplicial level.

Simplicial forms

  • There are a bunch of different (equivalent) definitions, but the idea is that they are forms that are well defined on the fat geometric realisation of a simplicial space.

  • The collection of degree-r simplicial forms on Y∙ is written ΩrY∙ and consists of sequence of forms

    ω∙=(ωp∈ΩYp×Δp∣(Y∙fip×id)∗ωp−1=(id×fip)∗ωp∈ΩYp×Δp−1)p∈ℕ
    where ΩYp×Δq consists of forms that are holomorphic on Yp and smooth on Δq.

  • An endomorphism-valued simplicial form on a simplicial vector bundle ∙ on Y∙ is a sequence of forms

    (ωp∈ΩYp×Δp⊗Ypnd(p⎯⎯⎯⎯))p∈ℕ such that(id⊗∙fip)((Y∙fip×id)∗⊗(Y∙fip)∗)ωp−1=((id×fip)∗⊗id)ωp(id⊗∙fip)∈ΩYp×Δp−1⊗om(p−1⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯,p⎯⎯⎯⎯)

    where we write q⎯⎯⎯⎯:=π∗qq for πq:Yq×Δq→Yq.

    This condition is really just saying that the form part gives a simplicial form, and that the endomorphism parts agree, i.e. for endomorphisms A on V and B on W and a morphism f:A→B we want that f∘A=B∘f∈Hom(A,B). Compare this to the fact that a morphism f is flat if ∇B∘f−f∘∇A=0.

Simplicial connections

  • A morphism ϕ:(E,∇E)→(F,∇F) of vector bundles with connections is said to be admissible if we can write
    EF=E1⊕E2=F1⊕F2

    such that
    1. ϕ:E/E1→F/F1 is a flat isomorphism; and
    2. E1 and F1 are flat (for their respective connections on E and F).
  • Let ∙ be a simplicial vector bundle. Write πp:Yp×Δp→Yp. Then a connection on p⎯⎯⎯⎯=π∗pp=p⊗YpYp×Δp is, of course, a map
    ∇p:p⎯⎯⎯⎯→p⎯⎯⎯⎯⊗YpΩ1Yp×Δp
    such that the Leibnitz rule holds.
  • Let ∇∙ be a collection of connections ∇p on p⎯⎯⎯⎯ and consider, for all face maps fip, the compositions
    Yp×Δp−1↓Y∙fip×idYp−1×Δp−1↓πp−1Yp−1−→−−−id×fipYp×Δp−→−πpYp

    and the morphism
    ∙fip:(Y∙fip)∗p−1→p
    of sheaves on Yp. Then consider the induced morphism
    (Y∙fip×id)∗(p−1⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯,∇p−1)→(id×fip)∗(p⎯⎯⎯⎯,∇p).

    We say that ∇∙ is a simplicial connection (on ∙) if this morphism is flat, and an admissible simplicial connection if this morphism is admissible. If ∙ comes with predetermined splittings
    (Y∙fip×id)∗p−1⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯⎯≅ℱp−1⊕𝒮p−1(id×fip)∗p⎯⎯⎯⎯≅𝒢p⊕𝒯p

    for all p, such that the above induced morphism is admissible with respect to this splitting, then we say that ∇∙ is a strongly admissible connection.

Motivation for definitions of admissibility

  • We have the following hierarchy of definitions:

    • A simplicial connection is defined exactly so that the difference between two connections is an endomorphism-valued simplicial 1-form;
    • An admissible simplicial connection is defined exactly so that we can evaluate invariant polynomials on the curvature (so that we can define characteristic classes);
    • A strongly admissible simplicial connection is defined exactly so that we can evaluate invariant polynomials on the endomorphism-valued 1-form part of the connection (so that we can apply transgression formulas).

    Lemma. Let ∇∙ and ∇′∙ be admissible (resp. strongly admissible) simplicial connections. Then K(∇∙) (resp. ∇′∙−∇∙) is an endomorphism-valued simplicial 2-form (resp 1-form).
