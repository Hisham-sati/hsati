BM96 via sheaf cohomology

Setup

  • π:E→X a holomorphic rank-r bundle;
  • f:Fk→X the bundle of (r−k+1)-frames of E along with a choice of complement, with fibre Wk homotopic to the Stiefel manifold Wnk;
  • the Deligne complex
    ℤ(p)=(2πi)pℤ↪X−→dΩ1X−→d…−→dΩp−1X
    where (2πi)pℤ is in degree zero;
  • sheaves (because we sheafify them) ℱp on X for p∈{0,…,2k} where
    ℱp(U)={σ:U×Δp→U×Wk≅f−1(U)∣fσ(x,t)=x} ;
  • homologically-graded complex
    𝒯∙=ℤX↪ℱ2k−1/ℱ2k−→∂ℱ2k−2−→∂…−→∂ℱ0
    where ℱ0 is in degree zero and ∂ is the usual boundary operator from the singular chain complex; the inclusion of ℤ into (ℱ2k−1/ℱ2k)(U) comes from the fact that kernel (under ∂) of the latter calculates the singular homology of Wk, which we know to be isomorphic to ℤ;
  • more generally, the local homology (i.e. the homology of a chain complex) is exactly the singular homology of the fibre, i.e.
    ℋi(𝒯∙)≅Hi(Wk;ℤ) for i∈{1,…,2k−1}
    and so 𝒯∙ is exact except at degree zero, giving us a quasi-isomorphism
    𝒯∙−→∼ℤX[0] ;
  • we have an element τ∈ℍ0(X;𝒯∙) corresponding to 1∈ℤ under the following chain of isomorphisms:
    ℤ≅H0(Wk;ℤ)≅ℍ0(Wk;ℤX[0])≅ℍ0(X;𝒯∙).

N.B. although we can do this, we prefer to not yet fibre integrate η∙, so it is, in what follows, a simplicial form (in these same degrees though).

    That is, Dη∙ is a lift of f∗ck to the Čech-de Rham complex, where D=d±δˇ.

Fibre integration

  • Generally, given a form ω of degree d on some U×G, and a p-cycle σ:U×Δp→G, we define fibre integration of ω over σ by
    ∫σω=∫Δpσ̃ ∗ω
    where σ̃ :U×Δp→U×G is given by the projection to U in the first coordinate, and σ in the second. The resulting form is of degree (d−p) on U, taken to be zero in the case p>d.

Goals

  1. Construct a map (i.e. degree-wise maps, but not necessarily giving a morphism of complexes) 𝒯∙→ℤ(2k)[2k] given by integrating cycles over some transgression of the k-th Chern class of E. Note that we sheafify 𝒯∙.
  2. Everything will be in the smooth case first, and to consider holomorphic things we will have to use the Hodge filtration.
  3. Show that, if E admits a global holomorphic connection, then this is indeed a morphism of complexes, and gives us a morphism 𝒯∙→ℍ2k(X;ℤ(2k)).
  4. Show that, if E does not admit a global holomorphic connection, then this fails to be a morphism of complexes (and that the obstruction is given exactly by the k-th Atiyah class?), but does give us a morphism of complexes 𝒯∙→ℍ2k(X;ℤ(k)).
  5. Explain why the image of τ in Deligne cohomology (in either case) under the respective morphism deserves to be called the kth Chern class. Do this by showing that what we construct agrees with BM96 when we take Čech?

The global case

  • Our transgression form η∙ is concentrated entirely in 𝒞ˇ0(Ω2k−1f∗E), and so we can think of it as a collection of forms ηα on f−1(Uα)≅Uα×Wk.
  • Fibre integration gives us a map
    ∫(−)(ηα)α∈I:ℱp→𝒞ˇ0(Ω2k−1−pX)
    for p∈{0,…,2k}, and we have the multiplication map ℤ→(2πi)pℤ, so to show that this gives a morphism of complexes we need to prove two things:
    1. fibre integration descends to a well-defined map on the quotient ℱ2k−1/ℱ2k;
    2. the squares all commute, i.e.
      D∘(∫(−)(ηα)α∈I)=(∫(−)(ηα)α∈I)∘d𝒯.
  • Lemma. Let Z∈ℱp for some p∈ℕ. Then
    D∫Z(ηα)α∈I=∫Z(Dηα)α∈I+∫∂Z(ηα)α∈I
