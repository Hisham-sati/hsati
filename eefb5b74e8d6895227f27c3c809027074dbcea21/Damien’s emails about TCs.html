Hi Tim,

for a general picture about A∞-categories, dg-categories and stable ∞-categories, you can have a look at some lecture notes of a course I gave (this is pure self-promotion): http://school2016.imj-prg.fr/Notes/Calaque_by_Rivera.pdf (only the first lecture is concerned with dg- and A∞-categories). by the way, Greg Ginot gave a great exercise session for this mini-course I gave. His notes shall be helpful... you should ask him ;-)

At the bottom of page 4 of these notes you'll find the definition of twisted objects in a dg-category.
This definition is exactly the same as the one of Bondal-Kapranov (I am referring to Definition 1 on page 95 of https://ncatlab.org/nlab/files/bondalKaprEnhTRiangCat.pdf ).
This definition generalizes to A∞-categories in an obvious way... the equation dδ+δ2=0 becomes ∑kmk(δ,...,δ)=0, where m1=d, m2 is the composition and higher mk's are higher homotopies for the associativity of composition (all mk's are the so-called A∞-structure). Keller may has a more abstract characterization of tw(A) but he also has an explicit description which is precisely what I said (up to sign issue that I like to ignore:-) ): see equation (7.1) on page 21 of https://arxiv.org/pdf/math/9910179.pdf

I guess the relation between 1/2 and 4/5 is that one shall apply 3/4/5 to the category of modules over the Cech resolution A=Cech(U,OX) of OX with respect to a given acyclic open cover U (A is a dg-algebra). I would guess that twisted objects for A in the sense of 3/4/5 are precisely the twisted guys of Toledo-Tong (which I'm not familiar with). Let me know if I'm wrong :)

...

(There is a fifth: Toledo, Tong 1986, where they work with ‘simplicial vector bundles’ – though these are not actually simplicial objects – but I’ve never heard of this approach used either before or after, so I don’t know if it’s too important).

This one is probably again equivalent.
Namely, the Cech resolution A of OX is actually the normalization of a cosimplicial algebra obtained as follows: the nerve of the cover U→X is a simplicial complex manifold, thus applying O (a contravariant functor) we get a cosimplical algebra. taking the so-called reduced (a-k-a Moore) complex associated with this cosimplicial algebra you get A.

Now a simplicial vector bundle is the data of a dg-vector bundle over each piece Ui of the cover that satisfy a bunch of gluing conditions up to d. So, its a kind of twisting module for the cosimplicial algebra we got...
Applying again a variant of Dold-Kan (the correspondence between (co)simplicial stuff and dg-stuff) one shall in the end get a twistingobject for A.

Sorry, these is not very precise, but I'm quite confident that this is the right thing.

Definition: [O'Brian, Toledo, Tong 1981]

Let \cover={Uα} be a "sufficiently nice" open cover of a paracompact complex analytic manifold X, and suppose that over each Uα we are given a graded holomorphic vector bundle End∙α={Endrα}r∈ℤ of finite length (globally bounded over all α, say).
Then a holomorphic twisting cochain consists of a cochain

\a=∑k⩾0\ak,1−k
with \ak,1−k∈Cˇk(\cover,End1−k(End)), such that:

  1. δ\a+\a⋅\a=0, and;
  2. \a1,0αα is chain homotopic[^1] to the identity for all α.

[^1]: The original definition actually requires that this map be the identity 'on the nose', but it turns out that this is too strong if we want to have mapping cones.

Definition: [Bondal, Kapranov 1991]

Let 𝒜 be a dg-category.
Then a twisted complex over 𝒜 is a set

{(Ei)i∈ℤ,qij:Ei→Ej}
where the Ei are objects in 𝒜, equal to zero for almost all i, and the qij are morphisms in 𝒜 of degree i−j+1 that satisfy the condition
dqij+∑k∈ℤqkjqik=0.

Example: [Bondal, Kapranov 1991]

If 𝒜=CDG(ℬ) is the category of complexes[^2] over an additive category ℬ, then a twisted complex over 𝒜 is simply a complex over ℬ in which each term has an additional grading.
This generalises the notion of a twisted complex over ℬ in the sense of [O'Brian, Toledo, Tong 1981].

[^2]: We make clear that this is the dg-category of complexes, i.e. a morphism between two complexes is just a collection of morphisms on each module – we impose no commutativity condition with the differentials.

Dear Tim,

let's start with the data of a graded holomorphic vector bundle α on every Uα. Let us write U=(Uα)α and =(α)α.

I think we do agree that E0:=C(U,) is a B-module, with B=C(U,OX) (just because on each Uα, α is a complex of Uα-modules... well, it is a graded Uα-module, which I decide to see as a complex with trivial differential).

Let me now put Ei=0 for every i≠0.

What does it mean then to say that (Ei)i has a twisted complex structure in the sense of [BK]?

It means that we have a degree 1 B-linear endomorphism a:=q00 of E0 such that da+aa=0.

But the dg-algebra of B-linear endomorphisms of E0 is precisely C(U,End()) !!!!
Thus a is a degree 1 element in C(U,End()) satisfying da+aa=0.
Then we decompose a as a sum (over k) of ak,1−k∈Ck(U,End1−k()).

So any [TT] holomorphic twisting cochain gives an example of a [BK] twisted complex in the dg-category B-mod (of dg-B-modules)

So, the main mistake not to do is to believe that there is a relation between the i's from [BK] and the k's from [TT] ;-)

In the end, holomorphic twisting cochains in the sense of [TT] are particular examples of twisted complexes in B-mod in the sense of [BK].
They are actually very special examples. For instance:

  • they are such that only one Ei is non-zero (while in [BK] finitely many may be non-zero).
  • they arrize as C(U,E), where U is graded. In Keller's context this may be similar to restricting oneself to those B-modules obtained as sums of shifts of free/projective B-modules.
  • there is no analog of the "normalization" condition 2 from [TT] in [BK].

I hope this helps.
