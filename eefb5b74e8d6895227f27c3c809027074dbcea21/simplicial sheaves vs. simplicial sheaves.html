Hi again,

let me add that:

  • in the nLab definition, one can replace simplicial sets with any other model category, for instance complexes.

  • in the Green/O'Brian/Toledo/Tong definition, one can replace Top with any other site (for instance the site of complex manifolds).

What I wrote still works very well in the context.

For instance, if Y is a complex manifolds and if X‚àó is the nerve of an open cover of Y.
Then one can show that |X‚àó| and Y are equivalent.

Hence ‚àû-sheaves of complexes on Y and on |X‚àó| are equivalent.
Hence any simplicial sheaf on X‚àó in the sense of Green/O'Brian/Toledo/Tong will give an ‚àû-sheaf of complexes on Y.
I guess these (homotopy sheaves of complexes on Y) are the objects of interest for us.
