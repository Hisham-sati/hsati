Green’s example (and more generality)

Resolution by simplicial vector bundles

Let X=ℙ1ℂ be the Riemann sphere ℂ∪{∞} with the (Stein) cover Uα=X∖{∞} and Uβ=X∖{0}, and let =𝕀({0}) be the sheaf of ideals corresponding to the subvariety {0}⊂X. Then =X/ is a coherent sheaf.

The stalks of  are simple to understand: x=0 for x≠0, and 0=ℂ. Thus, over Uα we have the resolution

ξ∙α=(0→X|Uα−→−−−f↦z⋅fX|Uα)→|Uα,

and over Uβ we have the resolution
ξ∙β=(0→0→0)→|Uβ.

If we want to define a simplicial vector bundle then we are going to need a resolution of αβ=0 by vector bundles over Uαβ, such that the simplicial conditions are satisfied. This gives us a problem however, since we have two different (but quasi-isomorphic, since 0,∞∉Uαβ, hence (z⋅) is an isomorphism) choices for such a complex:

(α)ξ∙αβ(β)ξ∙αβ=(0→X|Uαβ−→−−−f↦z⋅fX|Uαβ)→|Uαβ,=(0→0→0)→|Uαβ.

But we see that we can easily add the elementary sequence (α)(β)ℰ∙=(0→X→X→0) to the latter to obtain something isomorphic (since 0,∞∉Uαβ, so 1/z and z are well defined and holomorphic) to the former:
(α)ξ∙αβ=(0→(β)ξ∙αβ⊕(α)(β)ℰ∙=(0→X|Uαβ−→−−−f↦z⋅ff↦z⋅f↓X|Uαβ−→−−−idX|Uαβ)id↓X|Uαβ).

(See Simplicial vector bundles and twisting cochains for how finding such elementary sequences can be solved by the construction of a twisting cochain.)
We denote this isomorphism by A∙αβ:(α)ξ∙αβ−→∼(β)ξ∙αβ⊕(α)(β)ℰ∙, so that A0αβ=id and A1αβ=z⋅(−).

What is important is that we don't need this second complex to define the resolution by simplicial vector bundles, but instead to be able to pull back the local connection over Uβ to a connection over Uαβ in a non-trivial manner.

At this point, the construction stabilises: we get two complexes over Uαβγ, but they are identical (0→X|Uαβγ→X|Uαβγ). Further, since our cover consists of only two distinct opens sets, any n-intersection for n⩾3 will be trivially identical to some (possibly trivial) 2-intersection (either Uαα, Uαβ, or Uββ). Thus we have constructed a complex of simplicial vector bundles on the Čech nerve of X that give a resolution of  (pulled back trivially to the Čech nerve):

0→1∙→0∙→∙

where 1∙=0∙ is given by
i0onUα⊔UβisX|Uα⊔0i1onUαβisX|Uαβ

and the map 1∙→0∙ is given by
10X|Uα011X|Uαβ→00↦⋅zX|Uα↦0→01↦⋅zX|Uαβ.

Simplicial connections and the first Chern class

On each p-intersection Uα0…αp we get p+1 connections on ip, coming from the Uα0,…,Uαp (all pulled back to Uα0). Generally, given an isomorphism of vector bundles φ:E−→∼F with a connection ∇F on F, the pullback bundle φ∗∇F on E is given by (φ−1⊗φ∗)∘∇F∘φ. Locally, this takes the trivial connection d on F to d+φ−1dφ on E.

Now we can form the barycentric connections ∇i∙ on the i∙, where ∇i0=d, and

∇01∇11=t0d+t1d=d=t0d+t1(d+dzz)=d+t1dzz.

Looking only at the p=1 level (i.e. over Uαβ, which in this example is the only non-trivial bit), we calculate the curvature of these two simplicial connections:

K(∇01)K(∇11)=0=d(t1dzz)+(t1dzz)2=dt1dzz+t21(dzz)2.

attot(0∙)attot(1∙)=1=1+dzz.

Finally, we use the fact that, for a resolution ∙=(…→2∙→1∙) of a simplicial sheaf 0∙, the Chern character of 0∙ is given by the alternating sum of the Chern characters of the i∙ (this is just from K-theory and the Whitney sum formula), to see that

where the first equality is discussed in our construction of the simplicial Atiyah classes (also, see Chern classes, exponential Chern classes, the Chern character, and Atiyah classes).
